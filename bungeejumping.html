About Bungee Jumping

Relatively a new high adrenaline activity in India for bravehearts, it involves jumping from a height wherein your ankles are tied to a large elastic cord. Rishikesh offers the highest jump. Jumps take place from a cantilever platform constructed on a rocky bluff looking out over a tributary of the River Ganges.

When we free fall from a height, the cord stretches and rebounds, the momentum takes the jumper upwards and downwards. The up-and-down movement continues until the jumper comes to a standstill as all the energy from the elastic cord is exhausted. Although it looks terrifying, Bungee jumping is safe when conducted by trained professionals using reliable equipment.

Bungee Jumping Sites in India

  • North – Rishikesh (Uttarakhand), Delhi
  • South – Bangalore (Karnataka)
  • West – Anjuna Beach, North Goa (10km north of Calangute)

Physical fitness is a must. People with back problems, weak joints, high blood pressure, epilepsy, diabetes, neurological disorders, or pregnancy are advised against jumping. Weight range: 35–110 kg, Age: 14–50 (above 50 with instructor approval).

During strong winds and rains, jumps are avoided.
